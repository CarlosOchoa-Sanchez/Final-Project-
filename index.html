Plan the Perfect Weekend in North Carolina

A simple 3-day itinerary to explore Asheville, the Blue Ridge Parkway, and nearby mountain towns— all within easy driving distance.

Why Visit North Carolina?

North Carolina offers a diverse range of experiences, from scenic mountain overlooks to charming small towns and vibrant cities. This weekend guide focuses on Western North Carolina, with short drives between Asheville, the Blue Ridge Parkway, Chimney Rock, and Lake Lure.

Mountain Views

Enjoy sweeping vistas along the Blue Ridge Parkway, hike to waterfalls, and watch the sunset over the mountains.

Charming Towns

Explore downtown Asheville’s local shops, art galleries, and street performers in the heart of the mountains.

Food & Craft Beer

Taste Southern comfort food, farm-to-table restaurants, and award-winning breweries throughout Asheville and nearby towns.

Top Highlights in Western North Carolina

Downtown Asheville

Stroll through historic streets, local boutiques, street art, and live music—perfect for your first evening in the mountains.

Blue Ridge Parkway Overlooks

Take a scenic drive just outside Asheville and stop at overlooks for panoramic mountain views, especially near Craggy Gardens.

Pisgah National Forest

Hike to waterfalls like Looking Glass Falls or Moore Cove Falls, all within a reasonable drive from Asheville.

Chimney Rock & Lake Lure

Climb to the top of Chimney Rock for sweeping views, then relax by the water at Lake Lure—about an hour from Asheville.

3-Day North Carolina Mountain Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Asheville & Explore Downtown

  • Morning: Arrive in Asheville, check into your hotel or rental, and grab coffee at a local café downtown.
  • Afternoon: Walk through the River Arts District or browse local shops, bookstores, and art galleries.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner at a farm-to-table restaurant and end the night with live music or a rooftop view of the city.

Day 2: Blue Ridge Parkway & Pisgah National Forest

  • Morning: Drive a short distance from Asheville to the Blue Ridge Parkway and stop at a couple of overlooks near Craggy Gardens.
  • Afternoon: Continue toward Pisgah National Forest for an easy hike to a waterfall like Looking Glass Falls or Moore Cove Falls.
  • Evening: Head back to Asheville for a relaxed dinner and a sampler at one of the local breweries.

Day 3: Chimney Rock & Lake Lure

  • Morning: Drive to Chimney Rock (about an hour from Asheville) and hike to the top for sweeping views of Hickory Nut Gorge.
  • Afternoon: Spend time by the water at Lake Lure, rent a paddleboard, take a boat tour, or relax on the beach area.
  • Evening: Drive back to Asheville or begin your trip home with a final stop for barbecue or a casual dinner along the way.

Food & Drink Recommendations

Here are a few ideas to help you plan where to eat during your stay in Western North Carolina.

  • Barbecue & Southern Comfort: Try a local spot in Asheville or a roadside barbecue restaurant on your drive.
  • Farm-to-Table: Downtown Asheville is known for restaurants that highlight local produce and seasonal menus.
  • Cafés & Bakeries: Start your mornings with local coffee shops and bakeries before hitting the trails or the Parkway.
  • Craft Beer: Explore Asheville’s breweries in areas like the South Slope or the River Arts District.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Late spring, early summer, and early fall offer comfortable temperatures for hiking and scenic drives.
  • Getting Around: A car is the easiest way to explore Asheville, the Blue Ridge Parkway, Chimney Rock, and Lake Lure. Most drives are between 30 and 90 minutes.
  • What to Pack: Light layers, a rain jacket, comfortable walking or hiking shoes, sunscreen, and a reusable water bottle.
